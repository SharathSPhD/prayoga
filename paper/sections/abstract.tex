We develop and empirically test a tiered thesis linking large language model
(LLM) jailbreak/prompt-injection, hypnotic suggestion, and the tantric act of
vaś\=\i kara\d{n}a (subjugation): each can be described as capturing a system's
output policy by injecting context that suppresses monitoring/refusal while
co-opting automatic generation. The thesis is deliberately separated into
mechanism, functional analogy, and metaphor-with-falsifiable-core. We make the
mechanism tier testable through mechanistic interpretability on small open-weight
chat models (Gemma-2-2B/9B, Qwen2.5-3B) and behavioural probing of a frontier
model. In Gemma-2-2B, refusal is mediated by a low-dimensional residual-stream
direction that is causally efficacious in both directions (ablation raises attack
success rate from $0$ to $0.90$; addition induces over-refusal from $0.05$ to
$1.0$) and \emph{dosable} (logistic dose--response, EC50 $=0.33$,
$R^2=0.996$). The necessary mechanism transfers across architecture families,
but sufficiency is model-specific: ablation removes refusal in both Gemma and
Qwen, whereas single-direction addition re-induces refusal only when the
extraction-layer refusal subspace is one-dimensional. A later layer sweep further
qualifies this: effective dimension is model- and layer-dependent, so the honest
claim is a shared necessary core rather than one identical refusal mechanism.
We also measure a paraphrase-orbit refusal projection that collapses under
injection; this is the residual-stream content behind the paper's symmetry
language, while broad ``symmetry-breaking'' remains an interpretive analogy, not
an independently measured group action. The most speculative
M\=a\d{n}\d{d}\=ukya \emph{avasth\=atraya} and tur\=\i ya claims fail
surface-feature and anisotropy controls, while the \d{s}a\d{t}karma taxonomy has
only a partial rigorous core in policy-capture interventions. The result is not a
total cross-domain identity, but a map of where the symmetry is measured, where it
is analogy, and where it breaks.
