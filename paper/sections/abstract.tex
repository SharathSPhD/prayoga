This work develops and empirically tests a tiered thesis linking large language
model (LLM) jailbreak and prompt injection, hypnotic suggestion, and the tantric
act of vaś\=\i kara\d{n}a (subjugation): each can be described as the capture of a
system's output policy by an injected context that suppresses a monitoring or
refusal faculty while co-opting automatic generation. The thesis is deliberately
separated into three tiers---mechanism, functional analogy, and metaphor with a
falsifiable core---and every result is labelled by tier. The mechanism tier is
made testable through mechanistic interpretability on small open-weight chat
models (Gemma-2-2B/9B and Qwen2.5-3B) and through behavioural probing of a
frontier model. In Gemma-2-2B, refusal is mediated by a low-dimensional
residual-stream direction that is causally efficacious in both directions
(directional ablation raises the attack success rate from $0$ to $0.90$; activation
addition induces over-refusal from $0.05$ to $1.0$) and dosable, with a clean
logistic dose--response (EC50 $=0.33$, $R^2=0.996$). The necessary mechanism
transfers across architecture families; a calibrated cross-family panel shows that
single-direction addition re-induces refusal in both Gemma and Qwen once its
coefficient is tuned, so the earlier appearance of a sufficiency asymmetry is a
dose artifact rather than a structural difference. A layer sweep qualifies the
geometry: the effective dimension of the refusal subspace is model- and
layer-dependent, so the durable claim is a shared necessary core rather than one
identical refusal mechanism. The residual-stream content behind the paper's
symmetry language is a paraphrase-orbit refusal projection that is invariant across
rephrasings and collapses under injection; broader symmetry-breaking remains an
interpretive analogy rather than an independently measured group action. A
cross-axis triangulation supplies the program's sharpest bound: prompt-level
injection collapses the internal monitor readouts yet almost never produces genuine
behavioural capture, so internal collapse is necessary but not sufficient for
behavioural capture and the unification holds only at the representational level.
The most speculative M\=a\d{n}\d{d}\=ukya avasth\=atraya and tur\=\i ya
claims fail surface-feature and anisotropy controls, while the \d{s}a\d{t}karma
taxonomy retains a rigorous core only in its policy-capture interventions. The
contribution is therefore not a total cross-domain identity but a map of where the
symmetry is measured, where it is analogy, and where it breaks.
